### What I learned

### 🏛️ Deep Dive into Semantic HTML5

- **Semantic Markup Exploration:** Focused heavily on understanding the true programmatic meaning of HTML5 semantic elements to move away from generic layouts.
- **`article` vs `section`:** Documented and mastered the core difference between these two tags. I learned that an `<article>` represents completely independent, reusable content (like this entire preview card), whereas a `<section>` is for thematic grouping of related content within a page. I look forward to applying this structural decision-making to choose the right element for the right place in my next project.

### Continued development

In my future projects, I plan to focus on the following areas to continue growing as a developer:

- **Semantic HTML Decisions:** I want to build more complex layouts to practice identifying the right structural elements (like `<article>`, `<section>`, and `<aside>`) confidently from the very beginning.
- **Advanced Layouts with Flexbox & Grid:** While I am getting more comfortable with Flexbox for simple components, I want to master **CSS Grid** for full-page structures and more intricate multi-column designs.
- **Pixel-Perfect Typography:** I intend to practice more with fluid typography and advanced Google Fonts integration to ensure design match consistency across all device sizes.

### AI Collaboration

During this project, I collaborated with **Gemini** to elevate both my CSS layout skills and my understanding of modern HTML semantics.

- **Tools Used:** Gemini.
- **How I Used It:**
  - **Code Review & Semantics:** Used the assistant to review my HTML structure and deeply understand the programmatic difference between `<article>` and `<section>` through real-world analogies.

- **What Worked Well:** The AI acted as an adaptive mentor, breaking down complex semantic concepts into digestible explanations without giving away the final answers immediately. This interactive code review helped me learn _why_ certain structures are preferred over others.
